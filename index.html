Regenerative Law Foundation

The Regenerative Law Foundation seeks to advance regenerative legal frameworks that restore ecological systems and strengthen social cohesion. Guided by rigorous scientific research on planetary boundaries, ecological tipping points, and cumulative greenhouse gas attribution, we recognize that traditional notions of legal causality must be redefined to address complex global environmental challenges that defy simple cause-and-effect assumptions.

The Foundation aligns jurisprudence with these insights by shifting from linear cause-and-effect reasoning to models that recognize dynamic interdependence, cumulative risk, and probabilistic harm. Through constitutional argument and strategic litigation, we develop and apply these principles, reshaping the law to support regenerative outcomes for communities and the planet.
