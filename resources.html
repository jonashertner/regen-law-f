Scientific Research

Abram, N. J., Maher, N., Perkins-Kirkpatrick, S., Falster, G. M., Hughes, T. P., Meissner, K. J., Slater, L. J., King, A. D., Pitman, A. J., Moon, G., & Morgan, W. (2025). Quantifying the regional to global climate impacts of individual fossil fuel projects to inform decision-making. npj Climate Action, 4(92). https://doi.org/10.1038/s44168-025-00296-5

This groundbreaking study demonstrates how the Transient Climate Response to CO₂ Emissions (TCRE) enables quantification of additional global warming attributable to individual fossil fuel projects. The authors show that project-level emissions can be linked to concrete, foreseeable consequences—including human mortality, ecosystem damage, and exposure outside the human climate niche—providing an evidentiary foundation for proportional liability and informed decision-making in climate governance.

Key findings include methods for calculating warming contributions from project-level emissions, quantifying impacts on human populations (including heat-related deaths in Europe and populations exposed to unprecedented temperatures), and assessing ecological harm (such as coral loss on the Great Barrier Reef). The framework bridges climate science and legal decision-making by enabling risk assessments that move beyond claims of negligible impact to evidence-based evaluation of severe, long-term consequences.
